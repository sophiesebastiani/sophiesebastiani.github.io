Where's Your Soul? The Failure of Urban Renewal Fall 2022

Archival media project for Experimental Video Production at Wellesley College

An analysis of how the current (as of 2022) housing crisis facing the US stems from the highly racialized failure of the 1950's and 60's movement of urban renewal by all levels of the American government. Similar to my video essay critiquing the NFT craze, this work pulls much inspiration from Adam Curtis' documentary work, especially in creating historical narrative through the collage of moving images. The opening sequence of clips were filmed by me in Boston. The rest of the video incorporates appropriated footage, especially clips from contemporary news media, which I use to weave a visual timeline of the historical inequities in the housing market and address the cause of the same pattern of crashes in the market or great displacements like the one caused by the failure of this policy implementation.
