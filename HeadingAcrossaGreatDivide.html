    Heading Across a Great Divide Fall 2022 - Spring 2023

    "Somehow, you knew that everything was a bit odd, and a bit unreal, and often fake, but because there was no other picture of the world, and you were so within this system you accepted it as normal and just went on.‚Äù‚Äî HyperNormalisation explained by Adam Curtis

    During my senior year commute between MIT and Wellesley's respective campuses, I often found myself in Back Bay and the area surrounding Newbury Street. Lined with commercial buildings, the stores range from fast-fashion stores closest to Cambridge, like Urban Outfitters and TJ Maxx, and span toward luxury brands such as Cartier and Chanel who reside at the end of the road.

    Inspired by Baudrillard's concept of hyperreality, I visually explore how technology is narrowing the gap between reality and simulation through this project, to produce a surreal and generally unstable perception of the world around us. The structural inequalities in our society that maintain the social hierarchy of global capitalism have fractured the realities of many Americans by further widening the gap between the very wealthy and the poor, shrinking the middle class and decimating the american dream.
