Five Hundred Feet Away Winter 2022 - Spring 2023

This series is an introspective look into the environmental and social disruptions caused by the blossoming commercial and housing developments changing my hometown landscape from a primarily agricultural economy and its wealth of biodiversity, into a growing service industry dictated by private regional planning. The titular image is a collage of photographs captured along a county road in southern Alabama, where the construction of an Aldi grocery location occurs directly across the street from an established Walmart Supercenter, a few yards away from the body of a deer hit while crossing that same road. Using photomontage as a way to force an acute visual proximity between images creates a shocking dissonance between life and death, the natural and the constructed, with the heart of this project focusing on the residents of Baldwin and Mobile counties along Alabama's Gulf Coast adjusting to a shifting economy and ecosystem captured through candid and portrait photography. My work was displayed in the Panopticon Gallery in Boston from April 3rd to May 5th 2023, hosted by the Photographic Resource Center for their 2023 Student Show.
