NFTs and The Exploitation of Art Markets Spring 2022

Final Project + Paper (read here) for Decolonial Art History at Wellesley College. Video begins with a case against NFTs and the concept of speculative value in the art market through appropriated footage and follows with an introspection on the experience of viewing art (physically and digitally) + a conversation with a lovely gentleman who works as a security guard at the Davis about going to the museum. The paper critiques the artificial price inflation of digital objects within a speculative market and the fabrication of false realities that conceal real-world problems like rising poverty and homelessness in America and abroad.
