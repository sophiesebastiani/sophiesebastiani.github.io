Who is left behind as industry (and the political forces supporting it) only continue to accumulate wealth and power?

In my video work and in my photography, collage is something I enjoy experimenting with. Creating context, a visual narrative, through the spatial or sequential organization of static and moving images is so fun; I enjoy crafting a scene or series of clips that are densely-packed with information or feature small details that force the viewer to really pay attention, really look at the work.

I think playing with the digital medium (especially in post through digital software like photoshop) and with the camera itself, is something I prefer over the more manual process of analog film development (though I do enjoy that for the ritual of it). In my work, however, I want to create a very clear, sharply detailed image but providing a perspective that is entirely surreal. I want to get as close as possible to the lens of documentation because that is the language of online culture and manipulate the image at the same time.

Future me really wants to explore human interactions with user interfaces and how we can democratize user experiences to make online spaces more accessible and community-based, rather than fully subsumed into the monopolistic control of Big Tech.
