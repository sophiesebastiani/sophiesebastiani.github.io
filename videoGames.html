A Day Like Any Other Spring 2023


Unity 3D game made with Jeeyeon Barnes in Digital Worlds for Gaming. "On a day like any other, you recieve a letter." We were inspired by the Truman Show and the Stanley Parable to conceive of a world encapsulated within a perfectly identical suburb in which our main character lives, content with his mundane life with no connection to the real world until the illusion is broken. In creating the game's point of view, the fixed angles of the cameras create a feeling of surveillance that combined with the uncanny perfection of the outside world is already strange. This demo serves as the first part of a *theoretically* much larger game, which begins after the main camera switches from the fixed perspectives to a third-person follow cam, signifying that the main character has finally taken control of his life and is ready for the reality-breaking journey ahead of him.

Unity 3D game made with Janavi Padala in Digital Worlds for Gaming. Project Terra is a game where you land in the quarry of a mysterious world, with only the company of your loyal robot companion 2-T0R in your exploration. Once you complete the quarry tutorial stage, you decide to navigate to the only landmark in sight, an imposing museum in the middle of a dessert which holds a terrifying secret. My focus on the project was shaping the landscape and architecture of this world through 3D modeling, asset integration and lighting within the Unity 3D game engine.

Orwellian Industries: The Simulator Spring 2022

This is the day in the life of Orwellian Industries Employee #293915. You're a regular dude who just wants to chill and play a relaxing game on your five-minute daily break but the a.i. manager keeps getting on your dang nerves! >:( Ignore the system messages as fast as you can so you can get back to avoiding virtual falling office supplies and slurping down delicious Monsters (yum)!


Made in Unity 2D. I thought it would be interesting to create a meta-layer first between the player and the simulated player and then again with the simulated reality of the Employee character and the reality of a user *likely* playing this on a laptop similar to the one in the game. I created the background layer of an office worker's dirty desk (napkins, chip crumbs, empty cans of coke and Monster of course, etc.) and all of the ui elements (such as the Orwellian Industries title screen, system messages, game instructions, etc.), as well as the 2D character that moves back and forth across the 'Office Simulator' game in Photoshop.
