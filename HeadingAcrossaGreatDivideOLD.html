Heading Across a Great Divide Fall 2022 - Spring 2023

"Somehow, you knew that everything was a bit odd, and a bit unreal, and often fake, but because there was no other picture of the world, and you were so within this system you accepted it as normal and just went on.‚Äù‚Äî HyperNormalisation explained by Adam Curtis

Inspired by Baudrillard's concept of hyperreality, I visually explore how technology is narrowing the gap between reality and simulation through this project, to produce a very surreal and generally unstable perception of the world around us. The structural inequalities in our society that maintain the social hierarchy of global capitalism have fractured the realities of many Americans by further widening the gap between the very wealthy and the poor, shrinking the middle class and decimating the american dream. In large cities like Boston, the income disparities driving this hierarchy are obvious.

During my senior year commute between MIT and Wellesley's respective campuses, I often found myself in Back Bay, in the area surrounding Newbury Street. Lined with commercial buildings, the stores range from fast-fashion stores closest to Cambridge, like Urban Outfitters and TJ Maxx, and span toward luxury brands such as Cartier and Chanel who reside at the end of the road. Through this collection, I wanted to illustrate a perspective of the chaotic, absurd reality of the world while combining even further surreal aspects in photoshop to disorient the viewer. These images depict the containers of social inequity that we are all trapped in and our ignorance in the face of it, adding in visual themes of corporate development and an apocalypse of biblical proportions that is invisible to everyone in the scene while the viewer remains helpless to intervene. üåÄ Images featured in Remember a Place Not Here: Wellesley College's Spring 2023 Senior Art Exhibition
